Design Gap

Where the gap sits.

NMIA has a strong accessibility foundation through Passenger with Reduced Mobility (PRM) support, wheelchair assistance, and inclusive passenger services.

However, many elderly travellers don't identify as PRM. They are mobile and independent, but still experience travel anxiety, speed pressure, standing fatigue, digital confusion, and the fear of slowing others down.

The Need

Why age-friendly design matters.

NMIA is being built for decades of passenger growth as travelling populations in India and worldwide continue to age. This creates an opportunity to embed age-friendly journeys into the airport from the very beginning.

20M

Passengers NMIA is designed to serve annually.

17.2%

Maharashtra’s population projected to be aged 60+ by 2036.

230M

Indians projected to be aged 60+ by 2036.

1.4B

People globally projected to be aged 60+ by 2030.

Sources: Navi Mumbai International Airport; UNFPA India; GOI Population Projections; WHO.

Journey Friction

Transition points are always the hardest.

For elderly travellers, airport friction often appears at transition points: the moments where they need to decode what happens next.

Opportunity Statement

How might NMIA reduce uncertainty, effort, and pressure for older travellers across key airport transitions?

Opportunity Direction

Designing age-friendly airport journeys.

The following concepts explore how service, operational, and digital interventions could improve the airport experience across key points.

01

Senior Lane

An opt-in pathway for passengers 65+ who need more time, clearer guidance, and a lower-pressure journey.

Senior Lane imagesolution-nmia-senior-lane.jpg
02

Low-Friction Security

Uses advanced body scanners and millimeter-wave screening to reduce unnecessary pat-downs and help elderly passengers move through security with less effort.

Low-Friction Security imagesolution-nmia-calm-security.jpg
03

Personalised Journey View

A QR-based guide beside flight information screens that shows passengers only what they need next: gate details, directions, boarding status, and nearby help points. Helpful for all travellers.

NMIA Journey Mode UIsolution-nmia-journey-mode.png
04

Companion Access Pass

Allows one family member to accompany an older passenger through selected airport transition points where operationally feasible.

Companion Access Pass UIsolution-nmia-companion-pass.png
05

Pause Points

Rest zones positioned between major airport transitions, providing seating, water, wayfinding, and visible staff support.

Pause Points imagesolution-nmia-pause-points.jpg
